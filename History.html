Early History

Elgin's histroy goes all the way back to a 9th century pictish stone
The first stone of the cathedral was placed on the 19th of July in 1224. It was completed in 1242 and quickly burned down in a fire in 1270.
You can still visit the ruins today.
many famous kings of scotland had lodgings in elgin including David I, William I, Alexander II and Alexander III.
Edward I of England visited Elgin in 1296 in which he kept a journal which you can still view in Elgin Library.
When Robert the Bruce retook Scotland after Edwards death he destoryed many castles including the caslte on top of lady hill which you can still visit the ruins of.


Later History

If you visit Roseisle beach you will find these big square stones (seen left) and concrete bunkers along the coast line.
These were anti tank obsticles from WW2, if ever there was a landing from the north these would hopefully slow down the german infantry.
There is also evidence of American and Canadian practice runs of D-Day landings near Burghead.
